Popular Topics in Ethics

What is Ethics?

Ethics is the study of right and wrong, guiding how people should act and treat others. It explores moral principles, values, and what it means to live a good life.

Utilitarianism

The ethical theory that emphasizes maximizing happiness for the greatest number of people.

Immanuel Kant

German philosopher known for the categorical imperative and deontological ethics.

Virtue Ethics

An approach focusing on moral character rather than actions or their consequences.

Stoicism

Ancient philosophy emphasizing self-control and ethical conduct in harmony with nature.

Moral Relativism

The view that moral judgments are relative to particular standpoints and cultural contexts.

Aristotle

Ancient Greek philosopher who developed virtue ethics and the concept of eudaimonia.

Deontological Ethics

Ethical framework based on duty and moral rules rather than consequences.

Bioethics

Study of ethical issues emerging from advances in biology, medicine, and technology.

Peter Singer

Contemporary philosopher known for work in animal ethics and effective altruism.

Social Contract Theory

Theory that people's moral/political obligations depend on a contract among them to form society.

Existentialism

Philosophical movement emphasizing individual existence, freedom, and choice.

Environmental Ethics

Study of moral relationships between human beings and the environment.
